Workout programs

"Programs" are reusable to-do lists for your workout, so you can check off your exercises as you go and trigger pre-set rest timers. They're easy to create and share via e-mail, Messages, Facebook, Twitter or the Web. 

Here, we're sharing some of our favorite workout programs to get you started. If you're browsing this site on your iPhone, you can click through to add a workout program to your copy of Reps & Sets 1.4.

BEGINNERS

If you're new to weight resistance training, it's best to start with a balanced whole body approach, working all your major muscle groups in a single session that you can repeat three or four times a week. Aim for higher rep ranges (15-20 reps per set), to allow your body to get used to lifting, to avoid injury.

SPLITS

Split programs focus on different muscle groups on different days. This reduces the need for rest days, because one body area can recover while you're working on another. Focusing on muscle groups increases your training intensity which is important for endurance and hypertrophy goals.

GOALS

Whether you’re training for a physical challenge like a marathon, or aiming for the perfect beach body, goals add structure, focus and motivation to your workout. These programs utilise the full range of Reps & Sets features, including intervals, supersets, pyramids, dropsets and equipment settings.

Whole body program

This beginner's program is a great way to start at the gym. It starts with some cardio to get you warmed up, and then works through all the major muscle groups, using resistance machines rather than free weights, to help you get used to correct form. Adjust the weights so that you reach failure in your third set at around 15 reps. Each session should take about an hour. Complete this program 3-4 times a week, and always give yourself a day's rest between sessions.

Split programs focus on different muscle groups on different days. This reduces the need for rest days, because one body area can recover while you're working on another. Focusing on muscle groups increases your training intensity which is important for endurance and hypertrophy goals.

2 Day Split

This two day split program gives equal focus to upper and lower body, employing big, multi-joint lifts to develop functional strength. It's ideal for someone looking for something more challenging than a whole body approach program. Aim to complete both days twice a week with rest days in between.

Day 1: Upper BodyDay 2: Legs

3 Day Split

This three day split program is ideal for intermediate or advanced lifters with strength and hypertrophy goals. By combining muscle groups that normally work together when pushing, pulling or squatting, this balanced program includes plenty of big compound moves while also providing targeted intensity.

1: Chest, Delts & Triceps2: Back and Biceps3: Legs

5 Day Split

This five day split program is designed for an experienced gym user focusing on hypertrophy. By isolating a specific muscle group in each session, and using approaches such as pyramids, drop-sets and supersets, this program aims to completely exhaust each muscle group, to stimulate growth.

Marathon Mania

If you've committed to doing the old 42.2km, you've committed yourself to some serious training. Following a classic training pattern of lactic threshold training, tempo runs and a weekly long slow distance run this program might just help you reach the finish line. Follow the program for 12 weeks, steadily progressing distance or pace each week, then reduce training volume for a two week taper at the end.

Pectacular Bench Press

This program is designed to increase your one rep max on a classic test of strength – the bench press. For experience lifters, this program uses pyramids and drop-sets at low rep-ranges to completely exhaust the pecs, triceps and front delts. Select weights for which you reach failure in the specified reps, and aim to steadily progress weights each week. Complete once or twice a week, and remember to work your back, biceps and legs on other days.

Body Fat Blast

If you're aim is to reduce your body fat, then cutting the calories you consume is essential. But exercise also plays an important role - by burning more calories when you exercise, and increasing your basal metabolic rate, so you burn more calories when you're resting too. Combining HIIT and whole body weight resistance, aim to complete this workout program 3-4 times a week, and watch those calories burn.

Absolute abs - no gym required

Cutting body fat will reveal your six pack, but it won't make it bulge. Your rectus abdominis is much like any other muscle - if you want to make it bigger, you need to work it harder. So if you can see your six pack, but it's not much to look at, give this core workout a go. Hitting your abs from every angle and combining isometric (static) and isotonic (moving) exercises, this workout will not only add size to your six pack, it will increase your core strength as well.

The Chest Chiseller

A great pair of pecs is at the top of the to-do list for most guys at the gym. If you want thick, full pecs with a cleft in between like a valley, you'll need to be hitting them hard, and from every angle to make sure that your upper and lower pecs are all doing their fair share. Add this workout to a split program to give your chest some serious punishment.

Biceps may be the arm muscle that most guys focus on, but your triceps make up about two-third of our upper arm muscle mass, so they deserve some serious attention too. This workout combines pushing and pulling moves in the hypertrophy rep range to get your upper arms looking good from every angle. Work it into a split program on arm day and get your guns growing.

APPROACHES

When you’re looking to get past a plateau, advanced resistance approaches like supersets, pyramids and dropsets can make all the difference. That’s why, unlike most fitness apps, we have support for these build right in to Reps & Sets. These examples will show you how to include them in your programs.

Pyramid Scheme

This program demonstrates how you can build pyramids and reverse pyramids into a program. This is possible because Reps & Sets allows you to specify number of reps and weights for each individual set. By steadily increasing weight and reducing reps, pyramids have warmup built right in, and can help you pass a plateau in training by building towards heavier weights.

View Program

Drop Dead Drop Sets

By allowing you to specify rest intervals, Reps & Sets let you specify drop sets in a program. Dropsets provide a very intensive way of overloading your muscles. When you reach failure in a set, rather than resting, you drop the weight and continue until you reach failure again - usually repeating three times. When working with barbells, these are sometimes called strip sets because you strip the bar of plates as you go.

View Program

Super-duper Supersets

Reps & Sets allows you to combine multiple exercises to create supersets (you can specify three or more for tri-sets or giant sets). By combining exercises working opposing muscle groups, you can save time on resting. Alternatively, by hitting the same muscle with two different exercises you can increase training intensity by challenging the target muscle from different angles within a single set.
